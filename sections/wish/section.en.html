Objectives
WISH (Web Information System Hyperlink) is a project created with the objective of making the National Cave Registry of Italy accessible and consultable on the web through a portal built on a GIS platform.

WISH was proposed by the Speleological Federations of Campania and Veneto at the meeting (Permanent Table) of the Italian Speleological Society (SSI) and the Regional Speleological Federations (FSR) in Bologna on March 1, 2008; on that occasion, the SSI and FSR drafted a memorandum of understanding for the establishment of a Working Group for the definition of standards, tools and methodologies aimed at creating the Italian Caves Portal.

In fact, another important objective of WISH is to export experiences and best practices from one federation to another, bringing the registries to the same degree of computerization.

WISH is an ambitious project that aims to complete data for the entire national territory; it is in continuous evolution, it is an opportunity to standardize regional registries and a stimulus for Federations to make the registries work and "get networked" with other FSRs.
Structure
The portal allows navigation and consultation of data at the national level, search for general information and statistics, speleometric search, etc., while referring – through links – specific and detailed searches to regional webGIS, existing or being created.

The structure of WISH includes a portal where some types of searches can be made (e.g. speleometric searches, searches on special registries, etc.) at the national level; geographic searches can instead be performed from a regional to a municipal level; while, to obtain information and details beyond the municipal level, it will be necessary to access the individual webGIS sites of the FSRs participating in the project.

An in-depth consultation, the actual webGIS, will be made possible by a link that will redirect to the relevant regional webGIS, where present, with the characteristics, cartographic detail and information with which it was created.

The project is implementable and in the future it is planned to include other data related to: presence of tourist caves, caves classified as SCI (Sites of Community Importance) and those that fall into other special registries (polluted, destroyed, marine, etc.).
